  • Dartmouth Humanitarian Engineering

    Dartmouth Humanitarian Engineering (DHE) was founded in 2004 in response to the growing need for global poverty reduction and student demand for service and engineering opportunities abroad at Dartmouth College. Today, DHE is an award-winning, impact-driven organization that implements a wide range of technical projects in developing nations and aims to improve health and reduce environmental impact through sustainable, affordable,socially-conscious solutions.

    I am proud to be part of the DHE community. Having served as the organization's Director of Development from 2013-2015, authored and co-authored three peer-reviewed publications (here, here, and here), produced grant submissions and multimedia call-to-actions, and assisted with the preparation of technical documentation, I look forward to supporting DHE as an alumnus and continuing to track the organization's progress on their small-scale hydropower, locally-sourced bioenergy, and purified drinking water projects.

  • Goddard Riverside Community Center

    Goddard Riverside Community Center is one of New York City's leading human service organizations and is based in the Upper West Side. Goddard's mission is to "meet people's basic needs -- food, shelter, education -- and to bring them together for mutual aid, social action, and to celebrate our richness as a society."

    Since 2007, each Thanksgiving Day and Christmas Day, my family and I (and a couple hundred other volunteers) have worked with Goddard to host and serve a free, warm, home-cooked, sit-down holiday dinner. Volunteers help to prepare and serve over 3000 meals that are both given to visitors and delivered to home-bound locals who cannot attend the dinner. According to Goddard, "Guests include people in need, homeless adults, elderly individuals and families. The dinners have been a neighborhood tradition for more than 40 years."
